Dr.A.P.J.Abdul Kalam

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam (Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam) was an Indian scientist, engineer, and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he played a crucial role in India's civilian space program and military missile development.Kalam, often referred to as the "People's President," earned degrees in aeronautical engineering and joined the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). He played a pivotal role in the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle and ballistic missile system. Known as the "Missile Man of India," Dr. Kalam led successful projects such as Agni and Prithvi, contributing significantly to India's defense capabilities. His dedication to science and education extended beyond his scientific contributions. After his presidency, he continued to inspire students and young minds through lectures and writings, emphasizing the importance of education, innovation, and moral leadership.
